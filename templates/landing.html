1) Purpose of the Study

This study explores how people interact with online news articles and how different image labels influence readers' trust, perception, and decision-making.

2) What Will You Be Asked to Do?

You will be asked to read three news articles, select recommended follow-ups, and answer a few short questionnaires. The study takes approximately 10 minutes. There are no known risks or discomforts involved.

3) Data Privacy and Your Rights
  • Your participation is voluntary. You may withdraw at any time without giving a reason.
  • Your personal information (about who you are) remains confidential and will not be shared without your explicit consent. Your research data will be analyzed to answer the research question as described above in the goal of this study. Note that that further processing of your data is possible, provided that this is compatible with this purpose. Research data published in scientific journals will be anonymous and cannot be traced back to you as an individual. Finally: Completely anonymized data can be shared with other researchers and completely anonymized data can be made publicly accessible.
