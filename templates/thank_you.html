Thank you for participating!

We sincerely appreciate your time and input in our study.

Throughout the experiment, you were shown several news articles, some of which included an image transparency score label. This score was designed to help you assess how trustworthy and verifiable the images were.

The transparency score was based on factors such as:

  • Whether the image’s author is known,
  • If the image includes verifiable metadata (e.g., location, time, edits),
  • And whether there's a clear edit history or usage trace.

!!!!!You may have seen either a collapsed label (a small icon) or a fully expanded version with detailed explanation.!!!!! These variations were part of the study to explore how different forms of presentation influence how people perceive and engage with transparency information.

If some parts of the study felt unclear or inconsistent — that was expected. It helped us test how design and presentation affect understanding and choices.

Thank you again — your contribution helps us build better tools for transparency and trust in digital media.
