Thank you for your participation!

We sincerely appreciate your time and input in this study where we aimed to explore how people perceive and engage with transparency information.

Throughout the experiment, you were shown several news articles. Some of those might have included an image label. This label was designed to help you assess how trustworthy and verifiable the images were.

Participants were shown either no label or one of the different kinds of labels below. Using a variety of labels helped us test how design and presentation affect understanding and choices.

The transparency score was based on factors such as:

  • Whether the image’s author is known,
  • If the image includes verifiable metadata (e.g., location, time, edits),
  • And whether there's a clear edit history or usage trace.

You may have seen the label in a collapsed (small icon) and fully expanded version with detailed explanation.

Thank you again — your contribution helps us build better tools for transparency and trust in digital media.
